Background Story

Small businesses have always had a tough time reaching customers, as they have to compete with already established businesses, whose services and products are already trusted by customers.

These large established businesses are also easy to locate due to the extensive marketing budget that they have. This makes it harder for smaller businesses to compete with them because most people are not aware that these small businesses exist.

Small businesses and street vendors are losing sales due to covid. They were forced to close their doors during the initial 21 day lockdown, which was later extended. Even when certain small businesses were allowed to operate under being classified as an essential service. Most consumers carried on supporting larger businesses because they were easier to locate and could much easily enforce social distancing measures and give out sanitisers due to their pre existing capital and large store/shop space. Consumers are afraid of visiting small businesses because of overcrowding as they much often have smaller store/shop space.

These small businesses usually provide the same services/products that large businesses do, but often only gain access to a small market share. As most customers are not aware of the services that they offer, and thus miss out on the benefit of acquiring these services/products at a reduced cost.
